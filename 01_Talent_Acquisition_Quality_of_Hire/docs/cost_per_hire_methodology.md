# Cost-per-Hire Methodology Guide

## Overview

Cost-per-hire is a critical metric for understanding recruiting efficiency and ROI. This guide explains how to calculate and use cost-per-hire benchmarks for your organization.

---

## Standard Formula

```
Cost-per-Hire = (External Recruiting Costs + Internal Recruiting Costs) / Total Hires in Period
```

### External Costs Include:
- Job board postings and subscriptions
- Agency/recruiter fees
- Advertising and marketing
- Career fair and event costs
- Background checks and assessments
- Relocation expenses
- Referral bonuses

### Internal Costs Include:
- Recruiter salaries (pro-rated by time spent)
- Hiring manager time (interviews, reviews)
- Internal interview time across employees
- ATS/HRIS system costs
- Onboarding program costs

## Industry Benchmarks by Source

### 1. Employee Referrals
**Cost Range**: $1,000 - $3,500
**Average**: $1,500

**Cost Components**:
- Referral bonus: $500 - $2,500 (most common: $1,000)
- Administrative processing: $50 - $100
- Referral program marketing: $100 - $200
- Internal recruiter time: $300 - $700

**Factors Affecting Cost**:
- Bonus amount (entry-level vs. senior roles)
- Program maturity (established = more efficient)
- Industry competitiveness

**Quality Indicators**:
- ✅ Highest retention rates (referrals tend to stay longer)
- ✅ Faster time-to-productivity (cultural pre-screening)
- ✅ Higher performance ratings on average
- ⚠️ May reduce diversity (homophily bias)

---

### 2. LinkedIn
**Cost Range**: $3,000 - $6,000
**Average**: $4,000

**Cost Components**:
- LinkedIn Recruiter license: $8,000-$12,000/year
  - Divided by hires: $8,000 ÷ 20 hires = $400/hire (baseline)
- Recruiter time (sourcing, InMails): $1,500 - $2,500
- Job slot fees: $500 - $1,000
- Sponsored content: $500 - $1,500

**Factors Affecting Cost**:
- Seat utilization (hires per recruiter per year)
- Premium vs. Lite license
- Active sourcing vs. passive job postings

**Quality Indicators**:
- ✅ High-quality professional network
- ✅ Effective for mid-senior roles
- ⚠️ Competitive (everyone uses it)

---

### 3. Indeed
**Cost Range**: $500 - $3,500
**Average**: $2,000

**Cost Components**:
- Free posting: $0 (but low visibility)
- Sponsored posting: $150 - $500/month per job
- Pay-per-click: $0.25 - $2.00 per click
- Resume database access: $300/month (optional)
- Internal recruiter screening time: $400 - $1,000

**Cost Calculation Example**:
```
Job posting: 30 days
Sponsored at $5/day = $150
300 clicks @ $0.50 = $150
Total external: $300
Internal time: $800
Total: $1,100 per hire
```

**Factors Affecting Cost**:
- Job competitiveness (determines CPC)
- Sponsorship level
- Application volume vs. quality ratio

**Quality Indicators**:
- ✅ High volume for entry-mid level roles
- ⚠️ Variable quality (requires screening)
- ✅ Good for hourly/operational roles

### 4. Company Career Site (Direct Applicants)
**Cost Range**: $300 - $800
**Average**: $500

**Cost Components**:
- ATS amortized cost: $100 - $200
- Website hosting/maintenance: $50 - $100
- Internal recruiter screening: $200 - $500
- Employer branding content: $50 - $100

**Why Lowest Cost**:
- No external advertising spend
- Candidates already aware of company
- Direct applicants = strong interest signal

**Quality Indicators**:
- ✅ Pre-qualified (already researched company)
- ✅ Strong cultural fit likelihood
- ⚠️ Limited volume (depends on employer brand)

### 5. Recruiting Agencies
**Cost Range**: $12,000 - $25,000
**Average**: $18,000

**Cost Components**:
- Agency fee: **15-25% of first-year salary**
  - Entry-level ($50K salary): $7,500 - $12,500
  - Mid-level ($75K salary): $11,250 - $18,750
  - Senior ($120K salary): $18,000 - $30,000
- Internal coordination time: $500 - $1,000
- Background checks (if not included): $100 - $300

**Fee Structures**:
- **Contingency**: Pay only when hire is made (15-25%)
- **Retained**: Upfront payment + completion fee (20-33%)
- **Contract**: Hourly or project-based

**When to Use**:
- ✅ Hard-to-fill specialized roles
- ✅ Executive search
- ✅ Rapid scaling (need to fill quickly)
- ✅ Lack of internal recruiting capacity

**Quality Indicators**:
- ✅ Pre-vetted candidates
- ✅ Fastest time-to-fill
- ⚠️ Highest cost (but may be worth it for critical roles)

## How to Calculate YOUR Cost-per-Hire

### Step 1: Track All Costs for 3-6 Months

**External Costs (Easy to Track)**:
```
Job board subscriptions:     $5,000
Agency fees:                $30,000
Referral bonuses:            $8,000
Career fair costs:           $3,000
Background checks:           $2,000
Assessment tools:            $1,500
-----------------------------------
Total External:            $49,500
```

**Internal Costs (Requires Estimation)**:
```
Recruiter salaries (3 FTE @ $75K, 50% time on hiring):
  = 3 × $75,000 × 0.5 = $112,500

Hiring manager time (assume 50 managers, 10 hours each @ $75/hr):
  = 50 × 10 × $75 = $37,500

Interview panel time (200 interviews × 2 people × 1 hour @ $60/hr):
  = 200 × 2 × 60 = $24,000

ATS/HRIS costs:
  = $40,000/year ÷ 4 quarters = $10,000

-----------------------------------
Total Internal:           $184,000
```

**Total Cost-per-Hire**:
```
Total Recruiting Costs: $49,500 + $184,000 = $233,500
Total Hires in Period: 50
Cost-per-Hire: $233,500 ÷ 50 = $4,670
```

### Step 2: Allocate by Source

Track source-of-hire for each candidate, then allocate costs:

| Source | Hires | Specific Costs | Allocated Shared Costs | Total | Cost per Hire |

## ROI Calculation Methodology

### Quality of Hire Score (0-100)

```python
Quality Score = (
    (Performance Rating / 5) × 100 × 40% +  # 40% weight
    (Retention Rate) × 30% +                 # 30% weight
    (Manager Satisfaction / 5) × 100 × 30%   # 30% weight
)
```

**Example**:
- Performance: 4.2/5 → (4.2/5) × 100 = 84
- Retention: 92% → 92
- Manager Satisfaction: 4.5/5 → (4.5/5) × 100 = 90

```
Quality Score = (84 × 0.4) + (92 × 0.3) + (90 × 0.3)
              = 33.6 + 27.6 + 27
              = 88.2
```

### ROI Score

```
ROI Score = (Quality Score / Cost per Hire) × 1,000
```

**Why multiply by 1,000?**
Makes the score more readable. Instead of "0.031" you see "31".

**Example**:
- Referrals: Quality 88.2, Cost $2,800
  - ROI = (88.2 / 2,800) × 1,000 = **31.5**

- Agency: Quality 85, Cost $7,000
  - ROI = (85 / 7,000) × 1,000 = **12.1**

**Interpretation**:
- Referrals provide 2.6x better ROI than agencies
- Even though agencies provide high quality (85), the cost makes them less efficient

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

### ❌ Mistake 1: Not Including Internal Costs
Many companies only track external costs (job boards, agencies) and ignore recruiter time, hiring manager time, interview time.

**Impact**: Understates true cost by 50-70%

### ❌ Mistake 2: Using Industry Averages Without Adjustment
Your costs depend on:
- Company size
- Industry
- Location/market competitiveness
- Internal recruiting maturity

**Better**: Calculate YOUR actual costs, then compare to benchmarks.

### ❌ Mistake 3: Optimizing for Cost Alone
Cheapest source ≠ best source

**Better**: Optimize for ROI (quality per dollar)

### ❌ Mistake 4: Not Tracking by Source
If you don't know which source each hire came from, you can't optimize allocation.

**Better**: Mandatory source tagging in ATS.

### ❌ Mistake 5: Ignoring Hidden Costs
- Bad hire costs (30-50% of salary)
- Extended vacancy costs (0.5% of salary per day)
- Training costs for low-quality hires
