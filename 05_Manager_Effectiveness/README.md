# Manager Effectiveness

## Overview
Quantifies manager impact on engagement, retention, and team performance. Managers account for 70% of variance in team engagement (Gallup), making this one of the highest-leverage areas in people analytics.

## Business Impact
- Teams with highly effective managers have 50% lower turnover
- High-quality managers drive 3x higher engagement scores
- Top-tier managers develop 2x more high performers
- Best managers produce 3x more internal promotion candidates
- Manager training for identified weak managers yields 5:1 ROI through reduced attrition
- Google's Project Oxygen: Manager quality scores improved 75% through targeted development

## Key Metrics

**Manager Quality Score (composite):**
- Team Retention (30% weight) - Rolling 12-month voluntary attrition
- Team Engagement (25% weight) - Direct report survey scores
- Performance Distribution (20% weight) - % high performers, rating fairness
- Development Outcomes (15% weight) - Promotion rate, skill growth, mobility
- Leadership Behaviors (10% weight) - 1:1 frequency, feedback quality, coaching

**Scoring:**
- 9-10: Exceptional (Top 10%)
- 7-8: Strong (Next 20%)
- 5-6: Adequate (Middle 40%)
- 3-4: Struggling (Next 20%)
- 1-2: Ineffective (Bottom 10%)

**Behavioral Benchmarks:**
- 1:1 Frequency: Bi-weekly minimum, weekly ideal (HBR, 2023)
- Span of Control: 5-9 optimal for people managers (McKinsey, 2024)
- Team Engagement: >7.5 high-performing, <6.0 concern (Gallup Q12)
- Team Retention: >85% good, >90% excellent (Gallup, 2024)

## Notebooks

### 1. Manager Impact on Retention & Engagement
Identifies managers who retain vs lose talent.

**Key outputs:** Team retention by manager, engagement scores by team, regrettable attrition analysis, manager benchmarking

### 2. Manager Behaviors & Leadership Effectiveness
Connects observable behaviors to team outcomes.

**Key outputs:** 1:1 frequency analysis, coaching behavior correlation, performance feedback patterns, best practice identification

### 3. Manager Development ROI & High-Potential Identification
Quantifies whether manager training improves outcomes.

**Key outputs:** Pre/post training impact, engagement lift, retention improvement, high-potential manager identification

## Data Requirements
- HRIS: Manager-employee relationships, span of control, reporting structure
- Performance Management: Performance ratings, promotion history, PIPs
- Engagement Surveys: Team-level scores, manager effectiveness questions
- 1:1 Tracking: Meeting frequency and duration
- Attrition Data: Voluntary/involuntary exits, exit themes, regrettable flags
- Optional: 360 feedback, training data, productivity metrics

## Best Practices
- Use manager effectiveness dashboards for real-time visibility
- Target interventions based on data (specific development needs)
- Establish manager quality gates (don't promote ICs without leadership capability)
- Include people metrics in manager performance reviews and compensation
- Protect privacy (team-level data only, no individual employee exposure)
- Pair measurement with development resources
