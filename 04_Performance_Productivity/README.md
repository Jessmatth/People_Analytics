# Performance & Productivity

## Overview
Measures employee performance, improves performance management processes, calibrates ratings fairly, and optimizes workforce productivity. Helps identify top talent and improve organizational output.

## Business Impact
- Without performance analytics: Inconsistent ratings, high performers leave, low performers remain, subjective reviews
- With performance analytics: Calibrated ratings, early high potential identification, data-driven improvement plans, merit-based decisions
- Example: 500-person company addressing low performers 15 months faster saves $2.1M; retaining 7 additional high performers saves $700K annually

## Key Metrics
- Performance Rating Distribution (target: follows intended distribution) - Ensures calibration
- High Performer Retention (target: >90%) - Protect best talent
- Low Performer Exit Rate (target: >60% within 12 months) - Performance accountability
- Rating Consistency/ICC (target: 0.6-0.8) - Fair, calibrated ratings across managers
- Performance-Promotion Alignment (target: >80% from top tiers) - Merit-based advancement
- Performance-Pay Correlation (target: r > 0.5) - Pay for performance
- Productivity per Employee (revenue/headcount) - Efficiency measure

## Notebooks

### 1. Performance Ratings Distribution & Calibration
Ensures fair, consistent ratings across the organization.

**Key outputs:** Rating distribution by manager, manager variance metrics, demographic bias analysis, calibration dashboard

### 2. High Performer Identification & Retention
Identifies, develops, and retains top talent.

**Key outputs:** High performer profile, retention by performance tier, promotion rate analysis, flight risk overlay

### 3. Low Performer Management
Identifies and addresses underperformance quickly and fairly.

**Key outputs:** Low performer identification, time-to-action metrics, PIP outcomes, cost analysis

### 4. 9-Box Talent Matrix
Segments workforce by performance and potential for targeted development.

**Key outputs:** 9-box distribution, movement tracking, retention by segment, development plan completion

### 5. Performance Trends & Improvement Tracking
Tracks performance changes over time and measures improvement initiatives.

**Key outputs:** Performance trajectories (improving/stable/declining), development program impact, manager coaching effectiveness

### 6. Productivity Metrics & Drivers
Measures and optimizes workforce productivity.

**Key outputs:** Productivity trends, productivity by team/role, correlation with performance ratings, driver insights

## Data Requirements
- Performance Reviews: Ratings, review date, goals, comments
- HRIS: Department, manager, job level, tenure, demographics
- Compensation: Salary, bonus, market percentile
- Productivity Systems: Output metrics (tickets, deals, features, revenue)
- Talent Reviews: Potential rating, development plans, high potential flag

## Best Practices
- Define clear performance standards with behavioral examples
- Calibrate ratings across managers using data
- Act quickly on low performance (within 3-6 months)
- Retain and reward high performers (top 10-20% drive disproportionate value)
- Check for bias regularly by analyzing ratings by demographics
- Link performance to outcomes (promotions, compensation, development)
- Track performance over time (2-3 review cycles for patterns)
