# Organizational Design & Collaboration

## Overview
Analyzes organizational structure, spans of control, and collaboration patterns to optimize team design and cross-functional effectiveness.

## Business Impact
- Optimal spans of control (5-9 direct reports) correlate with 20% higher engagement
- Cross-functional collaboration drives knowledge sharing and innovation
- Each additional organizational layer adds 7% overhead cost
- Structural design shapes behavior, culture, and outcomes

## Key Metrics
- Span of Control (target: 5-9) - Number of direct reports per manager
- Organizational Layers - Levels between CEO and frontline (fewer = faster decisions)
- Cross-Functional Collaboration Score - Frequency and quality of inter-team work
- Collaboration Network Density - Strength of connections across teams
- Organizational Complexity Index - Structural inefficiency measure

## Notebooks

### 1. Spans of Control Analysis
Assesses manager-to-employee ratios and identifies structural inefficiencies.

**Key outputs:** Span distribution by level/department, comparison to optimal ranges, manager workload, organizational layer analysis

### 2. Collaboration Network Analysis
Maps cross-functional connections and identifies collaboration bottlenecks.

**Key outputs:** Network visualization, cross-departmental collaboration frequency, key connectors, silos and isolated teams

### 3. Organizational Efficiency Analysis
Assesses structural overhead and optimizes organizational complexity.

**Key outputs:** Manager overhead cost, complexity scoring, restructuring scenarios, decision-making speed analysis

## Data Requirements
- HRIS: Organizational hierarchy, manager-employee relationships
- Collaboration tools: Slack, email, meeting calendar data
- Project management systems: Cross-functional project participation
- Performance and innovation metrics

## Best Practices
- Use data to inform restructuring decisions
- Right-size teams for manager effectiveness
- Break down silos through targeted interventions
- Design scalable organizational structure for growth
- Communicate purpose of collaboration tracking to avoid surveillance concerns
